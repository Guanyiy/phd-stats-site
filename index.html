Guanyi Yang

Master’s Student in Applied Statistics
University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I study how cognitive data behave across diverse educational and cultural contexts, and how measurement and modeling choices shape the conclusions we draw from them. My work integrates applied statistics, psychometrics, and global aging research to improve the validity and interpretability of cognitive assessments.

Research Focus

  • Latent variable and multilevel modeling across global cohorts (HRS-HCAP, LASI-DAD, Mex-Cog, Nepal HCAP)
  • Statistical and psychometric methods for equitable and robust assessment
  • Cognitive aging and dementia measurement
  • Cross-cultural and educational adaptation of cognitive assessments
