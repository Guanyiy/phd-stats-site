Guanyi Yang

Master’s Student in Applied Statistics
University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I use data and statistical models to understand how people think, age, and adapt — and how measurement and context shape what we see in that data. My work combines the tools of applied statistics with questions from cognitive science and global health, aiming to make quantitative research both rigorous and human-centered.

Research Focus

  • Cognitive aging and dementia measurement
  • Cross-cultural and educational adaptation of neuropsychological tests
  • Latent variable and multilevel modeling across global cohorts (HRS-HCAP, LASI-DAD, Mex-Cog, Nepal HCAP)
  • Psychometric and statistical methods for fair and robust assessment in diverse populations
