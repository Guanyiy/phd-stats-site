üß© Overview#

This project contributes to the psychometric adaptation and validation of the Harmonized Cognitive Assessment Protocol (HCAP) for use in Nepal, a setting with substantial heterogeneity in literacy, language, and educational exposure.
We focus on refining visuospatial and attention measures‚Äîparticularly Constructional Praxis and Symbol Cancellation‚Äîto reduce floor effects, improve discriminant validity, and ensure cross-national comparability of cognitive functioning and MCI classification.

The broader goal is to build cognitive assessments that remain interpretable, reliable, and equitable in low-resource global aging contexts.

üìà Key Contributions#

  • Led analyses comparing adapted tasks with standard HCAP measures across literacy and education groups, demonstrating reduced floor effects and improved measurement precision
  • Evaluated discriminant validity using two-way ANOVA and Aligned Rank Transform (ART) ANOVA for non-normal outcomes
  • Conducted data cleaning, missingness diagnostics, and scoring harmonization in collaboration with field teams in Kathmandu and Chitwan
  • Integrated findings into the Nepal HCAP domain structure, informing downstream MCI modeling and factor scoring
  • Co-authored two abstracts (one first-authored) submitted to the 2026 International Neuropsychological Society (INS) Annual Meeting
