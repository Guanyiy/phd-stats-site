### ðŸ§© Overview  
This project builds a **cross-national statistical framework** for identifying predictors of **Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI)** subtypes using harmonized cognitive and health data from **HRS-HCAP**, **Mex-Cog**, and **LASI-DAD**.  
The work integrates:

- **latent variableâ€“based cognitive domain scores**,  
- **cross-national measurement harmonization**, and  
- **multinomial / ordinal regression modeling**,  

to evaluate how demographic, clinical, and psychosocial factors predict memory-impaired and non-memory-impaired MCI across heterogeneous populations.

A central focus is understanding **how measurement differences, floor effects, and literacy-linked missingness shape inference**, and developing models whose conclusions remain stable across cultural and educational contexts.

### ðŸ“ˆ Key Contributions  
- Harmonized demographic, clinical, and psychosocial predictors across three aging cohorts (age, rurality, vascular risk, depression, education).
